# Bibliography — CS 5463/4953 Term Project

## A Survey of Scalable Graph Neural Network Training Techniques on GPU Architectures

This bibliography lists foundational and recent conference/journal papers relevant to scalable Graph Neural Network (GNN) training, including sampling-based learning methods, graph partitioning strategies, distributed and multi-GPU training systems, and GPU-accelerated frameworks for large-scale graph learning.

---

## Category I: Foundational Papers

• **[F1]** Kipf, T. N., & Welling, M. (2017).  
Semi-Supervised Classification with Graph Convolutional Networks.  
*International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR 2017).*  
[Link](https://openreview.net/forum?id=SJU4ayYgl)

• **[F2]** Hamilton, W., Ying, Z., & Leskovec, J. (2017).  
Inductive Representation Learning on Large Graphs.  
*Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS 2017).*  
[Link](https://papers.nips.cc/paper/6703-inductive-representation-learning-on-large-graphs)

• **[F3]** Veličković, P., Cucurull, G., Casanova, A., Romero, A., Liò, P., & Bengio, Y. (2018).  
Graph Attention Networks.  
*International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR 2018).*  
[Link](https://openreview.net/forum?id=rJXMpikCZ)

• **[F4]** Xu, K., Hu, W., Leskovec, J., & Jegelka, S. (2019).  
How Powerful Are Graph Neural Networks?
